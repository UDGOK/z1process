## Black mass purity from mechanical processing has hard limits

Mechanically produced black mass from end-of-life EV batteries typically contains 1–10% copper, 1–5% aluminum, and 0.5–3% iron as impurities, alongside 33–50% graphite/carbon and the target cathode metals (Ni, Co, Mn, Li). These impurity levels are a fundamental consequence of incomplete liberation of electrode coatings from current collector foils during crushing. (ScienceDirect)

The equipment choice with the highest impact on purity is multi-stage comminution with intermediate screening. The critical mechanism is fracturing the PVDF binder that adheres cathode/anode active materials to their respective Al and Cu foils, then screening at  $ \sim500\mu m $ –1mm to capture liberated black mass powder while rejecting coarser foil fragments. A second crushing stage specifically targeting binder adhesion breakage — not just size reduction — is identified in the literature as the bottleneck of cathode active material recovery. Air classification is the second most impactful step, separating light foil and separator fragments from denser black mass particles. RecyclingInside Genoxtech

China's new GB/T 45203-2024 standard (effective July 2025) sets the first national specifications for recycled black mass. For Grade 1 NMC material, it requires  $ Ni+Co>35\% $ ,  $ Li>3.5\% $ , with  $ Cu<0.5\% $ ,  $ Al<1\% $ , and Fe<1%. (Fastmarkets) Meeting these impurity limits mechanically is achievable with production scrap (consistent chemistry, minimal casing contamination) but extremely challenging with mixed end-of-life batteries, particularly when steel-cased cells or LFP chemistry are in the feedstock mix. (ScienceDirect) EOL black mass typically trades at 60–75% payable against benchmark metal prices, versus 75–95% for production scrap — a direct reflection of purity differences. (Benchmarkminerals)

Feedstock chemistry matters enormously. NMC black mass (Ni 9–25%, Co 5–16%, Li 2–4%) commands the highest market value. (M-mines) LFP black mass contains no nickel or cobalt, making lithium the sole recoverable valuable metal — and the iron from LFP cathodes creates separation nightmares if mixed with NMC material. A buyer processing mixed feedstock should invest in upstream chemistry sorting (XRF-based or similar) to segregate NMC from LFP streams before mechanical processing. (Nature)

For the most aggressive CAPEX minimization regardless of geography, consider wet shredding (BCA Industries or Shred-Tech) to eliminate the discharge station entirely, combined with Chinese-sourced separation equipment. This reduces the total equipment count to approximately 5 major units and eliminates the most expensive safety engineering around inert gas management.

Regardless of equipment source, invest disproportionately in screening quality (multi-deck vibrating screens with precision cut points) and upstream feedstock sorting (by chemistry). These two relatively low-cost investments have the highest marginal impact on black mass purity and therefore on the revenue per ton of product — the metric that ultimately determines whether the plant's economics work at any CAPEX level.
