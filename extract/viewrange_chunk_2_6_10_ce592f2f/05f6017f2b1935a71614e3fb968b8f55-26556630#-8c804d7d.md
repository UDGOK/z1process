### 4. UPDATED COMPLETE SYSTEM DESIGN

### 4.1 Complete Process Flow

The updated battery recycling system now includes eight major process stages: (1) Battery Reception and Sorting - Incoming batteries are weighed, logged, and sorted by chemistry type (LFP, NMC, LCO, NCA) and form factor (cylindrical, prismatic, pouch). (2) Battery Discharge System - All batteries undergo controlled discharge to reduce cell voltage below 0.5V. Recommended: Salt water discharge for mixed/unknown types, automated conductive discharge for known types. (3) PACK Decomposition - Manual and semi-automated disassembly stations for EV modules, power tool packs, and laptop batteries. Includes emergency fire suppression and thermal monitoring. (4) Primary Shredding - Dual-shaft shredder with nitrogen inertization for initial size reduction to 20-50mm. (5) Secondary Crushing - Hammer mill or rotary shear for further size reduction to 5-10mm. (6) Drying and Electrolyte Evaporation - Heated conveyor or rotary kiln at 120-150 degrees C to evaporate remaining electrolyte. VOC emissions captured and routed to treatment. (7) Separation System - Vibrating screens, air classification, magnetic separation, and density separation to produce black mass, copper, aluminum, and plastic fractions. (8) VOC Treatment System - Regenerative thermal oxidizer (RTO) for destruction of volatile organic compounds from electrolyte evaporation.

### 5. RECOMMENDATIONS AND CONCLUSIONS

### 5.1 Recommended Configuration for Oklahoma Operation

Based on the engineer feedback, Oklahoma DEQ requirements, and cross-reference with Chinese LFP economics research, the following configuration is recommended for a 1 ton/hour mixed battery recycling operation in Oklahoma: (1) Battery Discharge - Hybrid approach with salt water discharge tanks for unknown/damaged cells and automated discharge station for known-type batteries. Estimated cost: $50,000-95,000. (2) PACK Decomposition - Three manual workstations with integrated fire suppression and thermal monitoring. PPE and training programs included. Estimated cost: $43,000-70,000. (3) Primary Shredding - BHS-Sonthofen VLR rotary shear (German quality, proven reliability) with nitrogen inertization. Estimated cost: $100,000-130,000. (4) Secondary Crushing - Chinese hammer mill (GEP ECOTECH or SUNY Group) for cost efficiency on non-critical equipment. Estimated cost: $40,000-60,000. (5) Drying System - Rotary kiln dryer with VOC capture hood, rated for 1 ton/hour. Estimated cost: $35,000-50,000. (6) VOC Treatment - Regenerative Thermal Oxidizer (RTO) sized for 5,000 CFM exhaust flow, 95% DRE guaranteed. Estimated cost: $180,000-220,000. (7) Separation System - Eriez magnetic separator, Chinese vibrating screens and air classifier. Estimated cost: $60,000-90,000. (8) Safety Systems - Nitrogen generation, gas detection, fire suppression throughout. Estimated cost: $65,000-100,000. Total Recommended CAPEX: $573,000-815,000 for complete system.

### 5.2 Oklahoma DEQ Permitting Strategy

For Oklahoma DEQ compliance, the following permitting strategy is recommended: (1) Pre-Application Meeting - Schedule a pre-application meeting with Oklahoma DEQ Air Quality Division to discuss the facility design, emission estimates, and permit pathway. (2) Synthetic Minor Permit - Design the facility with enforceable emissions limits below major source thresholds. With RTO achieving 95% DRE, VOC emissions should be below 10 tons/year. (3) Emissions Inventory - Prepare detailed emissions calculations for VOCs, particulate matter, and any HAPs from battery recycling operations. (4) BACT Analysis - Document Best Available Control Technology selection (RTO with 95% DRE) with supporting technical and economic analysis. (5) Emergency Response Plan - Develop a fire response and emergency evacuation plan specific to lithium battery hazards. (6) Timeline - Allow 6-12 months for air permit processing, with construction permit issued before equipment installation. Contact: Oklahoma DEQ Air Quality Division, 707 N. Robinson, Oklahoma City, OK 73102.

### 5.3 Critical Success Factors

The following factors are critical for successful operation: (1) Safety Culture - Battery recycling is inherently hazardous. Invest in training, PPE, and safety systems. One serious fire can destroy equipment worth hundreds of thousands of dollars. (2) Feedstock Quality - Economics depend on consistent feedstock. Secure contracts with battery collectors, auto dismantlers, and electronics recyclers. (3) Product Markets - Black mass buyers (hydrometallurgical processors) must be identified before production. LFP black mass has different value proposition than NMC black mass. (4) Regulatory Compliance - Work proactively with Oklahoma DEQ.

Violations can result in significant fines and operational shutdowns. (5) Technical Expertise - Retain a metallurgical engineer or experienced battery recycling consultant during design and commissioning phases. (6) Financial Reserves - Maintain adequate working capital for market fluctuations, especially lithium carbonate price volatility.

This updated recommendation addresses all engineer concerns while maintaining economic viability.
