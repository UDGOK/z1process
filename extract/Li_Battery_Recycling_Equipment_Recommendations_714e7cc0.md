## Alternative Reliable Manufacturers

While BHS-Sonthofen is the top recommendation, technology redundancy is essential for long-term operational security. The following manufacturers offer proven, reliable alternatives with demonstrated commercial deployments in lithium-ion battery recycling applications.

### 1. MTB Recycling (France)

MTB Recycling has developed specialized battery recycling equipment with a strong focus on safety and inert atmosphere processing. Their systems feature ATEX-compliant instrumentation and SIL 2 safety level components including temperature, pressure, and oxygen sensors. MTB offers complete lines for lithium battery processing with proven capability to handle EV battery packs, consumer

batteries, and production scrap. Their equipment is particularly noted for robust separation technology and compliance with European safety standards.

### 2. ANDRITZ Group (Austria/Germany) + Duesenfeld Partnership

ANDRITZ has partnered with Duesenfeld GmbH, a German pioneer in lithium-ion battery recycling, to commercialize an innovative low-temperature recycling process. The Duesenfeld process is notable for being the only recycling technology that does not require exhaust gas scrubbing, with electrolyte recovery as a key feature. This partnership combines ANDRITZ's industrial manufacturing capability with Duesenfeld's patented vacuum drying and mechanical separation technology. The process achieves 72% recycling rate in mechanical recycling alone, with potential for higher rates through hydrometallurgical processing.

### 3. Stokkermill (Italy)

Stokkermill offers complete lithium battery recycling plants with mechanical shredding and separation systems. Their equipment is designed for cost-effective operation and has been deployed in multiple European facilities. Stokkermill provides turnkey solutions ranging from small-scale pilot plants to full industrial installations. Their hammer mills and separation systems are particularly effective for mixed battery feed streams and offer good flexibility for varying input compositions.

### 4. Metso (Finland)

Metso provides comprehensive battery recycling solutions including their Battery Black Mass Recycling Process. While Metso focuses more on the hydrometallurgical processing of black mass, they also supply crushing and separation equipment for the mechanical pre-processing stage. Metso has supplied solvent extraction technology to Li-Cycle for their Rochester, New York facility, demonstrating their integration with major recyclers. Their equipment is known for reliability and integration with downstream processing.

## Key Research Insights

## What Major Recyclers Use:

Li-Cycle: Uses mechanical shredding technology to produce black mass, followed by hydrometallurgical processing. They have received solvent extraction technology from Metso for their Rochester, NY facility. Their approach emphasizes high recovery rates through optimized mechanical liberation.

Redwood Materials: Founded by former Tesla CTO JB Straubel, Redwood operates large-scale battery recycling in Nevada. They recycle EV production scrap for Panasonic and supply recovered materials back for new battery production. Their process focuses on recovering lithium, nickel, and cobalt at battery-grade purity.

BASF: Operates a BHS-Sonthofen-supplied plant for mechanical reprocessing of lithium-ion batteries into black mass. This represents a major validation of BHS equipment at industrial scale with one of the

world's largest chemical companies.

## Technology Trends:

Recent developments in battery recycling equipment have focused on enabling safe processing of batteries without prior discharge or disassembly. New systems from BHS-Sonthofen and other manufacturers can process the largest EV battery packs in a single system with appropriate inertization, significantly improving throughput. The industry is moving toward fully integrated lines that combine discharge, shredding, separation, and black mass collection in continuous processes, reducing manual handling and associated safety risks.

## Final Recommendations

1. For Maximum Reliability: Select BHS-Sonthofen rotary shear (VR) and universal shredder (NGU) for the primary and secondary crushing stages. This equipment has been validated at BASF's commercial-scale facility and offers the most robust inertizable design available.

2. For Technology Redundancy: Establish relationships with at least two equipment suppliers. MTB Recycling and Andritz/Duesenfeld provide viable alternatives with different technical approaches, ensuring supply chain resilience.

3. For Budget Optimization: Consider Chinese manufacturers (such as EcoNiLi or Suny Group) for secondary equipment where certification requirements permit. However, maintain Western equipment for critical shredding stages where safety systems integration is essential.

4. For Mixed Feedstock: Ensure equipment can handle your specific battery mix. EV packs require larger primary shredders with pack handling capability. Consumer electronics batteries can use smaller, finer shredding systems.

5. For Safety Compliance: Budget approximately 20-30% of equipment costs for safety systems integration including nitrogen inertization, fire suppression, gas detection, and emergency systems. This is not optional for safe operation.
