## 3. Process Flow: Modernized Recommendations

### Traditional 10-Step Process (Outdated)
1. Discharge → 2. Pre-sort → 3. Primary Shred → 4. Secondary Crush → 5. Fine Milling → 6. Screening → 7. Magnetic Sep. → 8. Eddy Current → 9. Air Classification → 10. Collection

### PhD-Recommended 6-Step Process (CAPEX-Optimized)
1. **Deep electrical discharge** with energy recovery
2. **Integrated shredding tower** under N₂ (eliminates primary + secondary as separate steps)
3. **Vacuum drying** at <80°C with electrolyte recovery (Duesenfeld-type)
4. **Screening** at ~1mm cut point
5. **Separation train**: magnetic → eddy current → air classification
6. **Black mass collection** (cyclone + baghouse)

**Key Changes:**
- Integrated shredding towers (ANDRITZ, ELDAN, BCA) achieve ≤15mm in single unit
- Fine milling often **eliminated** if shredding achieves adequate liberation
- Drying **before** separation improves downstream efficiency

| System | PhD Assessment | Implication |

## 6. Black Mass Quality: Realistic Expectations

### PhD Research Findings on Purity Limits

| Impurity | Typical Level | GB/T 45203-2024 Grade 1 Target |
|----------|---------------|-------------------------------|
| Copper | 1-10% | <0.5% |
| Aluminum | 1-5% | <1% |
| Iron | 0.5-3% | <1% |

**Key Insight:** Meeting Grade 1 standards (Ni+Co>35%, Li>3.5%) is:
- **Achievable** with production scrap (consistent chemistry)
- **Extremely challenging** with mixed end-of-life batteries

### Feedstock Chemistry Impact

| Chemistry | Black Mass Value | Processing Challenge |
|-----------|-----------------|---------------------|
| **NMC** | Highest (Ni 9-25%, Co 5-16%, Li 2-4%) | Standard processing |
| **LFP** | Lithium only | Iron creates separation issues |
| **Mixed** | Requires sorting | Must segregate NMC from LFP |

## 7. Consolidated Recommendations

### By Regulatory Environment

| Jurisdiction | Recommended Approach | Estimated CAPEX |
|--------------|---------------------|-----------------|
| **EU/ATEX-regulated** | ANDRITZ/Duesenfeld pathway | €5-12M |
| **Outside ATEX** (SE Asia, Middle East, Africa) | Chinese from Genox/Xingmao | $500K-$1.5M |
| **Aggressive CAPEX minimization** | Wet shredding + Chinese separation | $300K-$800K |

### Universal Recommendations

1. **Invest disproportionately in screening** - Multi-deck precision screens have highest marginal impact on purity
2. **Upstream chemistry sorting** - XRF-based sorting separates NMC from LFP before processing
3. **Don't eliminate fine milling entirely** - Only if shredding tower achieves adequate liberation
4. **Drying before separation** - PhD research confirms this improves downstream efficiency
